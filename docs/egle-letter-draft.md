Dear Air Quality Division staff,

I am writing on behalf of Planet Detroit, an independent nonprofit newsroom covering environmental and climate issues in Michigan. We are reaching out regarding the availability of Air Quality Division compliance and enforcement documents that were previously accessible through EGLE's Air Permits System (APS) at egle.state.mi.us/aps/downloads/SRN/.

**Background**

For several years, Planet Detroit and data journalist Shelby Jouppi maintained a public dashboard of air permit violations in Michigan, built on documents published to the APS system. The dashboard parsed violation notices, enforcement actions, and compliance evaluations from the APS document repository, making this public data more accessible to Michigan residents — particularly communities near major pollution sources.

We relied on the structured, predictable nature of the APS system: documents organized by State Registration Number (SRN), with filenames following the convention `{SRN}_{DOCTYPE}_{DATE}.pdf`. This allowed us to monitor for new violation notices and present them to the public in near-real time.

**What we've observed**

Beginning in approximately mid-to-late 2023, we noticed that new documents stopped appearing in the APS download directories for most facilities. While the APS system itself remains online and some historical documents are still accessible, the flow of new compliance and enforcement documents appears to have ceased.

Specifically:
- Our automated monitoring found no new violation notices after approximately August 2023
- The APS directory structure remains browsable, but most facility folders have not been updated
- We have been unable to determine whether this represents a policy change, a system migration, or a technical issue

**Our questions**

1. **Has EGLE discontinued publishing Air Quality Division documents to the APS download system?** If so, was this an intentional policy decision, and what was the rationale?

2. **Is there an alternative system where these documents are now being published?** We understand EGLE has been modernizing its data systems and want to make sure we're looking in the right place.

3. **Are violation notices, enforcement actions, compliance evaluations, and other AQD documents still considered public records?** We want to confirm that the underlying records remain accessible even if the delivery mechanism has changed.

4. **Is there a bulk access method or API for obtaining AQD documents?** We are building a new public tool (Pollution Near Me) that helps Michigan residents understand air quality compliance near their homes, and would like to incorporate EGLE's authoritative compliance data alongside EPA ECHO data.

5. **Would EGLE be open to discussing a more sustainable data-sharing arrangement?** For example, a regular data feed or API endpoint that provides structured compliance and enforcement data, which would reduce the need for scraping and ensure accuracy.

**Why this matters**

Michigan residents — especially those in frontline communities near industrial facilities — have a right to know about the compliance status of pollution sources in their neighborhoods. EPA's ECHO system provides some of this data, but EGLE's own records are more current and more detailed. The APS system was a valuable public resource, and its apparent discontinuation has created a gap in public access to environmental compliance information.

We are not writing to be adversarial. We recognize EGLE's commitment to transparency and understand that system transitions take time. We simply want to ensure that public access to these important records continues, and we'd like to work with you to find the best path forward.

We welcome the opportunity to discuss this further by phone or in a meeting.

Thank you for your time and your service to Michigan's environment.
